I am a sociologist and political scientist with two research tracks: as a scholar, I study Christian nationalism and how Americans imagine a "Christian nation" in fundamentally different ways. At PERIL, I design evidence-based approaches to prevent hate crimes and online extremism, including antisemitism, male supremacy, and gender-based violence.

Research Interests

My work bridges sociology of religion and political science to understand how religious identities intersect with nationalism, prejudice, and democratic attitudes in America.

Christian Nationalism

I developed a theoretical framework distinguishing Christian Statism (state-centric, ethno-exclusionary) from Religious Traditionalism (society-centric, color-blind). This duality explains divergent attitudes toward minorities, democracy, and violence.

Hate Crime and Online Extremism Prevention

At PERIL, I research what and how risk factors fuels radicalization and extremism and develop evidence-based and public health prevention strategies to prevent hate, bias, and extremist violence in communities across the United States.

Social Science Methodology

With ten years of experience of social sciences, I specialize in designing and analyzing large-scale surveys, survey experiment, (GIS) landscape analysis, and qualitative studies like focus group and participant interviews to measure and analyze complex religious, political, and cultural ideologies and behaviors with rigorous methodology.
